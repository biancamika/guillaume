Guillaumme de Machaut
Musician. Poet. Composer.

Biography

Guillaume de Machaut was born in Machault, a village in Champagne,which is near Reims and were most likely his surname is derived from. For an unknown specified amount of time, he served as a cleric and also served as a secretary to the then Count of Luxembourg and King of Bohemia John I from 1323-1346. With his king, he accompanied him on many trips to various European countries, many which were militaristic in nature.

He became canon in many places, such as Verdun in 1330, Reims in 1337 and Arras in 1332. He retired the job after Pope Benedict XII requested him to do so. After John I’s death in 1346, Guillaume was widely in demand, performing in many courts. Machaut luckily survived the infamous Black Death that swept Europe, outliving many of his patrons. He spent his last years in Reims, working on his manuscripts and other penned works. He died in 1377, widely mourned.

Musical Works

Trivias

All of Machaut’s music has been preserved in 32 manuscripts, representing a large part of the surviving music from his period. He was the first composer to write single-handedly a polyphonic setting of the mass ordinary, a work that has been recorded in modern performance. In most of this four-part setting he employs the characteristic Ars Nova technique of isorhythm(repeated overlapping of a rhythmic pattern in varying melodic forms).

The largest part of Machaut’s output was narrative or poetic. These works include the fifteen dits that he authored, along with the collection Loange des dames. His collection of chronicles amounts to about 40,000 lines of text. In addition, 142 of his musical works are still extant.

Musical Style

His musical style is Ars Nova/Medieval. Ars Nova is Medieval Latin for "New Art". Its innovative characteristics include the increased use of smaller note values. Machaut also wrote virelai, which belongs to this style; this is a French lyric poem of indefinite length composed of stanzas of long lines that rhyme, and short lines that rhyme. This era's composers are also known for creating music of increased rhythmic complexity.

Machaut's works also fall under the "motet" genre of medieval music. A motet is "polyphonic choral composition on a sacred text usually without instrumental accompaniment".

Messe de Nostre Dame

"Messe de Nostre Dame", or "The Mass of Our Lady" is his most famous work

. It a polyphonic mass divided into 5 movements, Kyrie, Gloria, Credo, Sanctus, and Agnus Dei, followed by the dismissal Ite, missa est. It also uses four voices instead of the typical three. Machaut added the contratenor, with the same low range as a tenor but sometimes replaces it as the lowest voice.

It was written as part of the commemoration of the Virgin endowed by the Machaut brothers at Rheims, and was intended for performance in a smaller setting by specialized soloists.

Douce Dame Jolie

"Douce Dame Jolie" or "Sweet, Beautiful Lady" is a virelai belonging to the style ars nova, and is one of the most often heard medieval tunes today.

The lyrics are romantic. The addressee is an unknown lady, said to be the object of the speaker's affections; he describes his love for her as so intense it could be considered a sickness.

Ma Fin Est Mon Commencement

"Ma Fin Est Mon Commencement", or "My End is My Beginning", is a polyphonic virelai that exhibits the bipartite (involving two separate parties) musical form and poetic layout "ABaAabAB" form (a and b represent two musical sections while the capital letters represent the refrain text)

His music also illustrated the meaning of his lyrics. For instance, the first two parts "My end is my beginning; my beginning is my end" have the same melody, but in opposite temporal directions.

Je vivroie liement/Liement me deport

"Je vivroie liement/Liement me deport", or "I Should Lead A Happy Life", is a polyphonic virelai.

The lyrics are romantic. The speaker in the song talks to the addressee (a young woman) about how his unnoticed affections make him want to die; they could only be cured by her.

J'aim sans penser

"J'aim sans penser", or "bla" is an instrumental of the Medieval style. The main instruments used here are bagpipes.
